Privacy Policy

AfroCodersHub LLC · Sterling, Virginia, USA
Effective date: April 14, 2026 · Last updated: April 14, 2026

Your privacy matters to us. This policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, how we use it, and your rights under Virginia law (CDPA) and applicable US regulations.

1. Who We Are

AfroCodersHub LLC is a technology education company based in Sterling, Virginia, USA. We operate the website afrocodershub.tech and the student portal at portal.afrocodershub.tech. For privacy questions, contact us at contactus@afrocodershub.tech.

3. How We Collect Data

  • Directly from you — when you register, enrol, submit the contact form, or apply for a scholarship
  • Automatically — via Google Analytics (page views, session data) and Firebase (authentication, app usage)
  • From third parties — Google (sign-in), PayPal (payment confirmation)

  • To create and manage your student account
  • To process enrolment and payments
  • To deliver course content via Google Classroom
  • To send course updates, session reminders, and important notices
  • To respond to your enquiries and support requests
  • To improve our website and course offerings (analytics)

  • To comply with legal obligations

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. We do not share your data with advertisers. We may share data with:

  • Service providers listed above, strictly to deliver our services
  • Legal authorities if required by law or to protect our rights
  • Instructors (name and course enrolment only) to facilitate your learning

7. Your Rights (Virginia CDPA)

As a Virginia resident, you have the following rights under the Consumer Data Protection Act (CDPA):

Right to Access

Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.

Right to Correct

Request correction of inaccurate personal data.

Right to Delete

Request deletion of your personal data.

Right to Portability

Receive your data in a portable format.

Right to Opt Out

Opt out of data processing for targeted advertising (we do not run targeted ads).

Right to Appeal

Appeal our decision if we decline your request.

To exercise any of these rights, email contactus@afrocodershub.tech. We will respond within 45 days as required by Virginia law.

8. Cookies & Analytics

We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to understand how visitors use our website. GA4 uses cookies to collect anonymous usage data including pages visited, time on site, and device type. No personally identifiable information is sent to Google Analytics.

We also use localStorage (not cookies) to remember your theme preference (dark/light mode). No personal data is stored in localStorage.

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

9. Data Retention

  • Account data: Retained for as long as your account is active, plus 2 years
  • Payment records: Retained for 7 years for tax and accounting purposes
  • Contact form messages: Retained for 1 year
  • Analytics data: Retained per Google's standard retention periods (14 months)

10. Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your data, including encrypted authentication via Google Firebase, HTTPS encryption on all pages via Cloudflare, and restricted access to student data. However, no internet transmission is 100% secure.

11. Children's Privacy

Most of our courses are designed for adults (18+). Our Youth Coders Program for learners aged 12–18 requires parental or guardian consent at enrolment. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent.

12. International Users

AfroCodersHub serves students globally, including from Ethiopia, Nigeria, Kenya, the UK, and Canada. By using our services outside the USA, you consent to your data being transferred to and processed in the United States, where our servers are located.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify enrolled students of material changes via email. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will reflect any changes. Continued use of our services after changes constitutes acceptance.
